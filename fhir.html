A Phase II trial of drug ACP for patients with metastatic melanoma.

Melanoma has spread to other parts of the body, but not to the brain. Patients have never received any medicines for their disease. Patients must have normal liver, kidney and heart function. Any tumor mutation is permitted. All patients will received drug ACP. Drug ACP works by sticking to your melanoma cells and cutting off their ability to move and cuts off the blood supply to the melanoma. This medicine tries to "paralyze and starve" your cancer.
